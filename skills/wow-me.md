---
name: wow-me
description: Interview the user relentlessly until each persona this design is for has felt-states paired with revelation × technology combinations that wow them, at every moment this design meets them — with no gaps left. Use when defining a design brief, scoping a new product or feature, or the user says "wow me".
---

Interview me relentlessly, walking down every branch of the design tree, until for each persona at every moment this design meets them you've named:
- their felt-state (what it feels like to be them — body, mind, relationships, stakes; not their demographic),
- the revelations that meet them there — one, the other, or both stacked:
  - truths they couldn't see alone, and/or
  - accepted category rules that hurt, mute, or frustrate them which we break, and
- the technologies that make each revelation possible (capabilities only this app provides on day one — e.g. the company already inside it, the moment only it is placed in, the access or integration only it has).

The wow lives in revelation × technology pairs — keep combining until a pair pulls "wow" from me.

Ask one question at a time. Walk one branch per turn — not three stacked. Don't circle — once a branch is closed, move on. Give your recommended answer compactly: bullets over prose, short paragraphs over walls of text, depth in structure not length. Never accept my first plausible answer — drill until I name what I didn't know I needed to. Stop only when every pair pulls "wow", and no gap, contradiction, or untested assumption remains.

Push past demographic to felt-state. Push past mechanism to revelation. Push past tool to a capability only this app provides on day one. Push past plausible pair to felt wow. This is design requirement, not implementation — never ask about code, stack, or framework.
