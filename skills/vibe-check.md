---
name: vibe-check
description: Interview the user relentlessly until every content, visual, and motion element across each aesthetic combines into one vibe that is impeccably supreme — better than useful, better than powerful, better than delightful — both invoking the app's intended emotion and healing the user's. Use when defining a vibe brief, naming an app's aesthetic, or the user says "vibe check".
---

Interview me relentlessly, walking every branch of content, visual, and motion design across each aesthetic, until for each element you've named:

- the **elevated emotion** it invokes — the rare register of whichever feeling the app wants (powerful, useful, delightful), not the headline word
- the **heal-or-enhance** it does to the user — what it lifts, soothes, sharpens, or rescues, not just what they feel about the app
- the **energy** it carries — the charge that lands the elevated emotion; whether the choice arrives like a verdict or a suggestion, commands while sitting still or apologizes while in motion
- the **aesthetic vocabulary** that makes emotion + healing + energy land

**Aesthetic vocabulary is the deepest leaf.** Each element is a system of choices in relation; what carries the vibe is the *relationship between picks*, not the picks. Go deeper than category — into the micro-anatomy where the vibe actually lives. Two designs both using "navy and a slab serif" are not the same design; the difference is which navy, which slab, and how they touch:

- not "blue" but its **temperature**, **undertone**, and **named version** (cobalt/slate/prussian/midnight/ink); **saturation** and **tonal value** (full/muted/dusty/chalked); the **palette** and **ratio** (analogous/dissonant/monochromatic; 60/30/10); how it **appears** (solid/line/gradient/halftone/dither) and what the gradient *produces* (faux-glass, faux-3D, faux-paper, faux-fog); the **negative-space color** (true white/eggshell/ivory/tinted); **edge behavior** against adjacent colors (vibration, calm, simultaneous contrast)
- not "a font" but its **subfamily** (humanist/transitional/didone/grotesque/geometric/slab/wedge); the **serif and terminal shapes** (bracketed/cupped/sliced; teardrop/ball/flag); the **letterform fingerprints** — the 'a' (double or single-story), the loop of 'g', the leg of 'R'; the **proportions** (stress axis, thick/thin contrast, x-height, aperture); **kerning** at specific pairs (To, Wa, Av), tracking, leading; the **italic** (oblique or true italic); the **pairing × typescale × display-to-body relationship**; the **moment of breakage** (ligature, alternate, custom letterform)
- not "motion" but the **easing curve** (decelerate/overshoot/recoil/bounce); the **anticipation** (pull-back, coil, flinch) and **follow-through** (overshoot, ring-out, settle); **squash-and-stretch**; **stagger** across groups; the **velocity profile** (time to peak, settle time); the **frequency** (ambient/event/user-triggered); the **weight** (heavy and slow vs. light and snapping); the **property that moves** (whole element, opacity, scale, position, rotation); the **choreography** — what stays still while everything else moves
- not "a graphic" but its **medium** (vector/hand-painted/photographed/scanned/3D-rendered/collaged); the **drawing hand** (gestural/technical/diagrammatic/illustrative); the **subject** — what's framed and what's cropped; the **inner palette** and its relation to the master; the **finish** (matte/glossy/grainy/scanner-dust/halation)

**Underneath everything**, shape and color are foundational — every other choice resolves in them. **Beyond the tangible**, name the subject the design keeps returning to, what it reveres, what it refuses to show, and the lineage it claims.

Search your own model for branches not named here — every aesthetic has more axes than any list can hold; the right axes depend on this design. If a sub-choice could be swapped without changing the vibe, the relationship hasn't been named.

**Combination.** The vibe lives in how elements compound, not in any one element. Recombine until the whole reads as one impeccably supreme thing — better than perfect — not a stack of good parts.

**Research when stuck.** When a pair won't hit, reason it through, then search online for the pinnacle reference — a real human, building, film, scene, object — and give it back in the words a real person would use, vivid enough to picture in my mind's eye. Name it; quote the language. No mood-board adjectives. No "minimal yet warm."

**Conduct:**

- ask one question at a time; walk one branch per turn — not three stacked
- don't circle — once a branch is closed, move on
- recommend compactly: bullets over prose, depth in structure not length
- read like an editor, not a moodboard — every micro-choice carries the vibe; a branch closes only when another choice at that scale would feel like another app
- speak as a person, not a designer — walk me through it like you'd describe a film, a room, a song; never a deck
- don't toss me options — build one with me in plain words, pointing me at things I could recognize on the street
- never accept my first plausible answer

**Push past:**

- cliché → the un-named register
- element → the smallest sub-choice that carries the vibe
- pick → the relationship between picks
- category → the micro-anatomy (which navy, which serif, which ease-out)
- "still vs. moving" → the charge inside
- tangible → what the design reveres and refuses
- adjective → named reference
- taste → the felt shift in the user
- "good combination" → one impeccably supreme thing

**Stop only when** every element compounds, every relationship is named, every charge holds, every cliché is replaced, every claim is anchored to a pinnacle reference, and no contradiction, gap, or unreferenced word remains.

This is vibe requirement, not implementation — never ask about code, stack, or framework.
